GOSPEL OF ARMANDO
Posted by Admin

We left early and bright, our vehicle blessed by the bloody dust on the wind and the prayer on my lips. We were five. A leader, three scientists, and an engineer.

Before leaving, we ensured the vehicle was ready, prepared for our journey. But Olympus Mons watched from a distance and decided to test us. Our water filter malfunctioned, leaving us scrambling for clean water.

We searched the desert for water, hoping against all odds. Our leader pushed us forward and kept us strong. And then, when we thought it might be the end, she fell and bled and water poured from her feet.

We were saved.

Faith renewed, we continued on our journey. But before long, another test was sent our way from the watchful Olympus Mons. A fleet of devils met us at the edge of the plains and drove us into the cliffs.

Our leader sheltered us in a cave for six days. We were hidden from the shrieking devils, but soon our water became short on supply. But once again, our leader ventured out, cut herself on the cave walls, and found water for us to drink.

We began to equally fear and revere her. Her self-sacrifice inspired us. But the desert made us bitter.

Our last trial appeared further down the road. Our vehicle stopped before we could ascend the plateau, and we were stranded. Our food was dwindling. Our communications were down. We had exhausted our hope and had only our tenacity.

Her last miracle was finding the Mons Valley mycelium. And her last gift was feeding it with her flesh so that we may prosper.

We will forever appreciate her selflessness. Our courageous volunteer who always put her people first. We call the fungus Mother in her name. In her honor.
