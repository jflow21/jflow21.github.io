GOSPEL OF LIR
Posted by Admin
Day 0
I say goodbye to my Shayla and boys today for the third time. I reassure them that I'll be back before they know it. My youngest is mad that I've been called to an expedition again. And last time was supposed to be the last time. I try to console him, but at the end of the day, nothing I say will be enough for the poor lad.
Day 1
After checking everything is in working condition, I'm introduced to our expedition leader V. She seems alright now that I've met her in person, a strong, good leader. Next, I met our geologist, A. He seems reserved but detail-oriented. Good work ethic. I meet P & J, the chemist and biologist and their lab assistant Nik. We set out by midday.
Day 3
All life support systems are functional, in good shape. No substantial change from the initial check.
Day 4
Rover is doing grand. She should hold up for the rest of our trip. V is mapping out our course.
Day 6
A is excited about all the rocks he's been picking up. I had a refreshing chat with him. He's got a good head on his shoulders.
Day 7
It's getting hot in here. I mess with the AC and get it working a little harder. King keeps moping around, mouth a'gaping, or hiding. I fix the AC to keep him cool.
Day 8

The fucking water filter. I knew she was trouble. I must be an dumbass, course something was off. Feck. I ought to tell V.

—--------------------------------

V is hopping mad. Honest to god, I am too. There's two days' worth of water before a recycling cycle kicks in. If I can't get this fixed in the next few days, we're going to have to start recycling in other ways, but even that may not be enough to get us all the way back. Might need to find ice instead. Fuck.

—--------------------------------

So why in hell did the system not trigger a warning days ago before jus crapping out. It seems one of the pressure pumps is blown. It smells awful. After further inspection of the system, that seems to be the only problem. I rerouted the system to only use the functional pump and ignore the failed one. It's not perfect - filtration will be half as efficient - but it'll do. It's just a band-aid to our problem, I suspect an electrical surge somewhere upstream.

Day 9
The second motor fails We're gonna have to refuel on water about every 10 days from now on.
Day 10

I guess we're just gonna die out here. I ought to send a message to Shayla. Say my last goodbye.

—-----------------------------------------

I can't believe my eyes. N just stumbled upon water??? I can't say I'm not relieved. They've saved us.

Day 12
N asked for help with an experimental setup.
Day 15
A dust devil kicked up quite a fuss. The whole region's been enveloped. V drove us to park in a cave but knocked the damn satelite on our way in. I hope to hell that SOS made it back to base.
Day 16
Even King's got himself all worked up. I've been on this shit all day but no amount of work or prayer can bring this fucker back to a decent shape. Even if the electronics aren't done for, this dish won't ever get the signal out, ok.
Day 19
Running low on water again. I don't know what were gonna do if we have to continue on like this the whole goddamn time.
Day 20
N found more water deeper into the cave. We'll be aight then for definite.
Day 21
The Devils calm down today. We head out and continue on our way with the new stash of ice.
Day 25
Food's gonna be short from now on if we want to make it back all in one piece. A could stand to lose a kilo or two. I need to keep my strength up since I'm keeping the rover in good shape, so I don't think I should get my rations cut at all.
Day 27
V doesn't respect the Rover for her life. If she did, she'd've known the rover's filter was getting clogged. She's out here yelling at me for not catching it, but this kind of thing is a driver's responsibility. You have to be one with the car, listen to the engine, and respect her limits. She doesn't seem to understand that not every system on a car needs a goddamn sensor. The filtration system is much too damaged to keep going now.
Day 30
The egg-heads brought some kind of mushroom on board. Maybe there's hope afterall if there's actually native martian life.
Day 35
P & J say it's edible. If this is also a source of food, the mother will have saved all of our lives.
Day 37
P & J have been whispering these days. It feels different, not so silly anymore. They say we might need to feed the mushrooms to get us through to the end. I got my families ill be damned if its me to go first. N says they want to volunteer.
Day 40

We spent the last few days trying to convince N not to sacrifice themselves, but in the end it was their choice.

N became one with the mother today. “Go n-éirí an bóthar leat” brother.

Day 43
V is going to set off. No way she makes it on her own.
Day 45
A is beside himself with grief. I can't understand it. It was the best decision for the likes of us.
Day 51
King's gotten out, but none of us really tried to stop em.
Day 57

Dear Shayla,

I'm sorry for leaving you and the lads to manage on your own again. I know how heavy the load falls on you when I'm off chasing pumps in this blasted red dust—Tommy's questions about “when's Da home?” ringing through my head every damn day. I wish I could be there to see Sean score that winning goal or to help tuck them in at night.

I think of you all every time the rover shudders or the comms console lights up—your faces are the only thing that keeps me steady when I'm out here.

I promise I'll haul my boots back across the void as soon as this job's done.

All my love,

Lir

Day 60

By God, we're saved. I though we were right fucking goners. My boys will have their father again. They'll grow happy and healthy. I'll get to hear my dear wife's voice again.

I'll be honest, I thought I'd never make it back. Thank the mother that we'll make it.
