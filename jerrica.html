GOSPEL OF JERRICA
Posted by Admin
Day 0

Personal notes:

My skills in manipulating the genome and my abilities to medically support my compatriots has earned me a place on the Western Expedition. I will miss my home, but the new challenge excites me
Day 1

Personnel Information

  • Vera
  • Armando
  • Lir
  • Puja
  • Nik

Personal notes:

My team is interesting. Nik proves to be a mystery, Lir stubborn, Puja reserved, Armando timid, and Vera headstrong.

Some here refuse to share information necessary to their health. I find this frustrating. How are we to trust each other with secrets such as this?

Day 5

Personal notes:

The blood of the King was found throughout our vehicle. Armando ensured he would prevent this kind of mess again.

Lir continues to refuse testing. His stubbornness will be his end.

Day 8

Personal notes:

We have lost the purity of our water. We shall continue on, searching for a new source. I shall need to check the wellbeing of our crew regularly given the added stress.

My morning ritual of tea calls to me. I do miss it.

Day 10

Personal notes:

Our water has been replenished. Life may continue, so we trek on to save our people.

Day 12

Personal notes:

Idles hands make my mind wander. The others have explicit tasks, but I do not. It leaves me feeling… empty.

Day 14

Personal notes:

The others are worried about dust devils on the horizon. I trust Lir to get us through this mess, but I'm not sure Vera will hold up under this pressure

Day 15

Personal notes:

I underestimated the strength of the storm. The cabin rattles and lurches constantly. It is ever-present. I have taken to triple checking the environmental conditions to stave off my rising anxieties.

Day 16

Personal notes:

Our air filter falters. We can still breathe, but our engine cannot. Lir disappoints, but I worry he will punish himself too harshly. I should keep a watchful eye on him.

Day 24

Personal notes:

The first of what I imagine to be many tough conversations happened today. We're going to be forced to begin more strict food rationing. I will need to keep a constant eye on people's vitals to reduce risk.

Day 27

Personal notes:

Our home is stopped. All the others are coping in their own ways. We need to come together though. This separation does us no good.

I also worry Puja is not eating well. Her food goes untouched despite my protestations.

Day 30

Personal notes:

We found something! The Mons Mycelia. A fungus that responds to stimulus remarkably well. It's presence here is a miracle and could save us all.

Day 31

Personal notes:

It can be eaten! We shall feed these to the King before consuming them ourselves. I hope this proves to be the salvation we need.

Day 35

Personal notes:

The Mons Mycelia consumes and processes materials into biomass faster than anything I've ever seen! It appears to gather most of its energy by breaking down various wavelengths of radiation, making the areas around if safer for human habitation. It also spreads like kudzu across the lost cities of Earth. I imagine it could be used to help create a safe colony space for the long term.

Additionally, It shows a remarkable level of genetic flexibility. It appears to be able to copy and utilize genetic information from other lifeforms. This was discovered when some of the samples I had been feeding King appeared to metastasize into a growth in the tank. King appeared to be unaffected at worst, and thriving at best. The key detail is that the mushroom had a growth covered in scales identical to those on King.

I just hope the thing doesn't take to spraying blood like its genetic benefactor.

Day 37

Personal notes:

We're almost out of food, but Puja and I had an idea. None of us will make it home as is. But we can await rescue.

The only way we make it that long though is with the mushroom. It would need a substantial chunk of biomass, but providing this could allow me to direct the growth to or near our RV to help maintain a habitable environment. I am testing this with King's habitat soon.

Finding out the mechanisms it uses to copy and use DNA is fascinating. It changes everything.

Day 40

Personal notes:

A decision had to be made. With King's new habitat proving the viability of my idea, we knew we had to feed it. Everyone but Nik agreed. The question came down to who.

Nik volunteered. She had no family, no career, not future here. She saw that her sacrifice would save so many and chose to do so. Lir and I helped facilitate the transition. We shall see if this is enough.

Rest in Peace, Nik.

Day 45

Personal notes:

The Mons Mycelia is much easier to shape than I had expected. It is as if I can talk to it. Our temporary habitat is set up, with one less occupant than expected. Vera lost herself to the guilt of not being able to save everyone and wandered into the wastes. I expect they won't make it past tomorrow.

Nik is dead so we can live. We should honor her, not sully her memory with tragedy.

Day 50

Personal notes:

I can feel the Mons Mycelia at the fringes of my thoughts. It was listening? It doesn't “talk” but does provide subtle signals. I don't know how to explain it other than a hand guiding my path but not determining it. If controlled, this effect could massively shape the efficiency of our colony.

We have the opportunity to truly become something greater together. Cells to a larger body. A comforting thought.

I have taken to calling it “The Mother” with its guidance.

Day ??

Personal notes:

We're back. The others almost didn't believe us about the Mons Mycelia. They believed our recounts of how the others fell though, with much sadness expressed at the martyrdom of Nik and Vera.

Some of the more socially influential people are discussing mobilizing the colony to try and rebuild around the mushroom. I will join them. We shall carve out a new future together, united under her lordship, the Mother.
